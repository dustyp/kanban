What is Agile?

Agile is a group of software development methods in which requirements and solutions evolve through collaboration between self-organizing cross-functional teams.

What are Sprints (Scrum)?

Agile Sprints (Scrum) is a style of agile software development that focuses on iteration or incremental construction through time-boxed cycles of planning and review.

What is Kanban?

Kanban is a continuous flow of work through a defined workflow controlled by Work In Progress (WIP) limits applied to each step. Work is pulled based on available capacity.

In all cases the ultimate objective is: Always provide the development team with the best* thing to work on next, no more and no less.

*Best = (Most valuable | important | impact)

Kaban is well suited when:

  • Work tends to switch context or priority rapidly and frequently
  • Work is complicated by several systems, workflows, stakeholders
  • Continuous delivery mechanisms are in place to match the flow of work through the system
  • Workflow tends to be serial and is well defined
  • Team is capable of swarming when WIP is hit

US Content Team

  • Scrumban
  • Produt Roadmap + Technical Debt + Emergent Sales Requests + Maintenance
  • Scrum failed to really achieve intended purpose. Sprints lacked focus and felt unsustainable
  • Move to Kanban reduced time spent in long meetings, forced local ownership due to continuous planning
  • Multiple swim lanes made work easy to visualize. WIP forced reality of throughput.
  • Manage a responsive redesign and improved codebase while keeping highest sales throughput ever.

Moving to Kanban from Scrum

  • Scrum-ban - handle tasks in scrum as kanban and use retros to adjust WIP
  • Create any missing work agreements for workflow(s), Task WIP and Definitions of Done
  • Invest in process improvement to improve flow (Traffic Jams and Air Bubbles)
  • Board visualization is important. Its right in the name. Start analog if co-located.
  • Get serious about cards and the board. Board tells an accurate story.
  • Incrementally remove the need for time-boxes and meeting cadence.
  • Stop estimating!
  • Work with Product to manage the flow of cards into Kanbans.

Shared Services, Dependencies & Stakeholders

  • Regular Dependencies = longer flow. Extend the board.
  • Remove dependencies through advisory roles that empower teams with skills and automation (Invest)
  • Stakeholders may still want planning to control decision making. Metrics and visible boards help make teams transparent.
  • Roll-up teams and products into portfolio kanbans
  • Stand-ups in front of the board are still public.
  • Demos to pull in stakeholder feedback

Kanban Mistakes are Agile Mistakes

  • Cards with bad definition or no definition of done
  • "I did my job" = Waiting
  • QA as the gate of all testing and quality = Flow imbalance
  • Assigning work to specialists in the backlog = Artificial bottlenecks
  • All waiting is bad waiting. Inventory your waiting.
  • Planning too far ahead = wasted time. Roadmaps are as mythical as project plans.
  • Lack of team metrics means endless churn and no visibility to improve
  • Board is not up-to-date. Teams not motivated to kill cards.

Principles for Success

  • Autonomy, Mastery & Purpose
  • Understand and measure value.
  • Teams depend on trust, respect and clear communication.
  • Eliminate waiting while focusing on sustainable quality.
